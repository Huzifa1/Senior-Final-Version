Food Zone

In Time Out, We have experts and nutritionists who follow up on the health of prisoners, provide them with health advice, and help them choose the appropriate meal that suits their bodies on the one hand and their health status on the other. In addition to our keenness on health and food, we know that the place of food provides the prisoner with psychological comfort, which in turn affects his physical health.
The main meals in our restaurant consist of the following characteristics:

High protein (Animal and Plant sources): Protein provides nutrients that are vital for an inmate’s health because they are packed with “building blocks” for bones, muscles, cartilage, skin, and blood.Is a nutrient that helps other body processes like creating enzymes, regulating hormones, and supplying vitamins, which is crucial for maintenance of the body.

Fruits and Vegetables: People who eat whole grains as part of a healthy diet have a reduced risk of some chronic diseases, heart disease and constipation. Sticking to the recommended 6-ounces will help with weight management.

Smart Snacking: Snacking is almost always inevitable, and that's all right because a healthy snack between meals in prison can actually help overeating, decrease your hunger and speed up metabolism. Staying active is just as important as making healthy food choices. Snacking can be an important part of a diet when an inmate is active, because of the added energy boost. It is OK to have an unhealthy snack once in awhile. Never allowing any unhealthy snacks or sweets may result in sneaking these foods. The key is balance and moderation!
